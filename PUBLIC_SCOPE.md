# Public Disclosure Scope

**Project:** From Biomass to Biochemical Yield
**Version:** 0.1.0
**Status:** Public disclosure policy

## 1. Purpose

This document defines which project information may be disclosed through this public repository.

The objective is to provide a credible, transparent and citable scientific overview without publishing confidential, proprietary, personal or insufficiently validated material.

## 2. Information authorized for public disclosure

The following information may be published:

* project title and research objective;
* public research questions;
* scientific definitions and equations;
* high-level experimental architecture;
* selected species, cultivar and harvested organ;
* candidate analytes;
* families of environmental variables;
* non-confidential reproducibility principles;
* high-level research roadmap;
* methodological limitations;
* version history;
* public references;
* public datasets and scripts after review.

## 3. Information excluded from public disclosure

The following information must not be published without a specific review and authorization:

### 3.1 Proprietary operational material

* detailed substrate recipes;
* detailed fertigation formulations;
* exact cultivation recipes;
* proprietary environmental setpoint sequences;
* complete spectral recipes;
* detailed automation scripts;
* credentials, tokens, URLs containing private identifiers or infrastructure details.

### 3.2 Personal and organizational information

* private email addresses;
* telephone numbers;
* home or experimental-site addresses;
* identity documents;
* personal data concerning collaborators;
* names or roles of contributors without their consent;
* unconfirmed institutional affiliations.

### 3.3 Unvalidated scientific statements

* claims of controller superiority;
* claimed biochemical gains;
* claimed energy or water savings;
* claimed medical, therapeutic or nutritional effects;
* claims of industrial scalability;
* simulated or estimated results presented as experimental observations.

### 3.4 Restricted documents

* complete competition or funding applications;
* internal audit reports;
* confidential partner discussions;
* unpublished commercial terms;
* documents subject to third-party copyright restrictions.

## 4. Evidence-status labels

Public technical statements should use one of the following labels where relevant:

* **INSTALLED** — physically present in the experimental platform;
* **CALIBRATED** — measured or validated against a documented procedure;
* **PROPOSED** — planned but not yet implemented or validated;
* **OBSERVED** — directly derived from recorded experimental data;
* **INFERRED** — analytical interpretation supported by stated assumptions;
* **EXTERNAL EVIDENCE** — supported by an identified external source.

An installed device must not automatically be described as calibrated.

A proposed method must not be described as an observed result.

## 5. Publication rule

Before publication, each new file must be checked for:

1. personal data;
2. confidential operational information;
3. unsupported claims;
4. third-party copyrighted content;
5. hidden metadata;
6. credentials or access tokens;
7. names of contributors without consent;
8. inconsistencies with the current canonical project version.

## 6. Versioning

Any material change to the research question, biological system, endpoint hierarchy, experimental unit or analytical panel must:

* receive a new version number;
* be dated;
* state what changed;
* state why it changed;
* preserve the previous public version through repository history.

## 7. Current disclosure decision

Version 0.1.0 publishes only:

* the scientific framing;
* the core biochemical-yield equation;
* the bounded initial pilot concept;
* the reproducibility principles;
* the research roadmap;
* the project limitations.

No experimental data, operational recipe or performance result is included.
